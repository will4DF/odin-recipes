No Bake Biscoff Cheesecake Bars

Home

Description

This is the easiest most affordable, quickest, simplest, yummiest, best recipe desert you'll ever find. Enjoy!

Ingredients

Biscoff Cookie Crust

  • 2 cups (200 g) biscoff cookie crumbs 25 cookies
  • 5 tablespoon butter melted and cooled
  • Pinch of salt

Bicoff Cheesecake

  • ¾ cup (178.5 g) heavy whipping cream
  • 12 oz (340.2 g) cream cheese softened
  • ⅓ cup (66.67 g) granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla
  • ½ teaspoon (0.5 teaspoon) cinnamon
  • ¼ teaspoon (0.25 teaspoon) salt
  • ¾ cup (177.44 g) biscoff cookie butter

Bicoff Tooping

  • ½ cup (118.29 g) biscoff cookie butter
  • 3 (3-4) biscoff cookies crushed or broken into pieces

Steps

To make the crust

  1. Place biscoff cookies in a food processor and blend until no cookie chunks are left. Add pinch of salt and melted butter and pulse until combined.
  2. Pour into the bottom of your baking pan and press down to create a firm and even crust layer. Freeze or refrigerate for about 20 minutes.

Biscoff Cheesecake

  1. In a large mixing bowl, whip your whipping cream until stiff peaks are formed. Set aside while you assemble the rest.
  2. In another large bowl beat the cream cheese until soft. Then beat in the sugar, vanilla, cinnamon and salt.
  3. Add cookie butter and mix on low until all combined. Fold in whipping cream and stir until all mixed together.
  4. Remove pan from freezer and pour the cheesecake over the chilled crust. Cover with saran wrap and refrigerate for at least 6 hours, but I usually like to leave it over night.
  5. Add ½ cup of cookie butter to a microwave safe bowl and warm for about 15-20 seconds, or until warm enough to pour. Pour over chilled cheesecake and then place back in the fridge for about a half hour to set up.
  6. Top with cookie crumbs or pieces for serving.
